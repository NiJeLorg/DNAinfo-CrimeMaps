How This Map Was Made

With thousands of DNAinfo visitors having drawn the boundaries of their neighborhoods, we wanted to give a sense of where there was broad agreement (and disagreement) in where neighborhoods begin and end. To this end, we randomly selected up to 50 drawings from DNAinfo visitors for this neighborhood. Using these edges of these overlapping shapes, we created a collection of non-overlapping polygons. These non-overlapping polygons are what you see above. The final step is to count how many of the shapes DNAinfo visitors drew representing this neighborhood fall within our new non-overlapping polygons. We then colorize this map above based on the number of times visitors drew a shape that fell within each polygon.
